Basic Rules of Ping Pong

Want to play ping pong but dont know where to start?

Here is a summary of essential rules for playing!

Ping pong is a fast-paced game, where the goal is to score points by making shots your opponent cannot return according to the rules. Players have to follow specifics for serving, scoring, gameplay to keep matches fair. This page will help settle those garage ping pong disputes.

1. Games Are Played to 11 Points

Games are played to 11 points. If tied at 11 points, you must win by two points. A match is generally a best three of five games

2. Alternate Who Serves Every Two Points

Each player alternates serving every two points played.
EXCEPTION: Tied 10-10 (deuce), service alternates every point played until a player wins by two points.

3. Open Palm When Tossing The Ball Up to Serve

The ball must be served from an open palm, behind your end of the table. The ball must go at least 6 inches straight up, and ball path must be visible to the opposing player. The ball has to your side of the table and then the opponents side.
NOTE: Once the ball leaves the server's hand it is in play. If the server misses the ball on serve, it is the reciever's point.

4. Serves That Touch The Net On The Way Over is Called a "Let"

The ball is only allowed to touch the net during a rally. When rallying and the ball touches the top of the net and lands on the opposite side of the table, its a legitimate hit. When Serving, if the ball hits the net on the way over, it is a "let" and the serve has to be redone. There is no limit on how many times this happens,

5. Singles Play: The Ball Can Land Anywhere on Serve

The ball can land anywhere on either side of the table during a serve when playing singles. The ball is allowed to bounce more than once on the opponent's side, bounce over the side the of the table and even hit the edge of the table.
NOTE: A double bounce on the opponents side of the table results in the returner's point

6. Doubles Play: Serve Must Go Right Court to Right Court

Serving during doubles play, the ball must bounce in the server's right court, and reciever's right court. Think that the ball has to go diagonally across the table to be in play off serve in doubles. Doubles partners switch places after their team serves twice.
NOTE: Landing on the center line is fair.

7. Doubles Play: Alternate Who Hits The Ball in a Doubles Rally

Doubles partners must alternate hitting the ball during rally regardless of where the ball lands on their side of the table. Players must be ready to hit the ball and move to allow the partner to take the next returning ball.

8. Volleys Are Not Allowed

The ball must bounce on your side of the table before you return it back to your opponent. If you hit the ball before it bounces on your side of the table, it results in the opponent earning a point.
NOTE: If the opponent hits the ball over your end of the table without touching it or it hits you or your paddle, it is your point. The ball must bounce and the reciever mis-hits or does not get the ball over the net, for it to be the opponent's point.

9. If Your Return Bounces Back Over The Net By Itself, It Is Your Point

If the ball is hit during rally or a serve and it bounces back over the net AFTER hitting the opponent's side of the table, without your opponent touching the ball, its the returner's point.

10. Touching The Ball With Your "Paddle" Hand Is Allowed

The ball can touch the hand that is holding the paddle. There is no violation and play shall continue as normal. This means the ball can touch your fingers, and hand area below the wrist.

11. You Cannot Touch The ball or Table With Your Non-Paddle Hand

You may not touch the ball with your non-paddle hand for any reason. This will result in the opponent earning a point. Only the paddle hand, the paddle, or other parts of your body can touch the table.
Example: reaching in to return a short ball.
NOTE: If the table is moved at all from being trouched during a rally, it will be the opponents point.

12. Top Edge of the Table Still Counts

Legal serves and hits may contact the top edge of the horizontal table top surface and be valid hits, even if it bounces sideways.
NOTE: The vertical sides of the table are NOT part of the legal playing surface.

13. Honor System

If you are playing at a shop, your garage, or a randomly placed ping pong table with no referee present during the match, the "honor system" applies and the players should find a way to agree OR play the point over.
