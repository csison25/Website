Teambuilding

Why Teambuild?

Pokémon VGC battles are filled with strategic decisions. Having a well designed team that functions as a cohesive unit with a clear strategy will help you win battles. Unlike casual play, where you can rely solely on your favorite or strongest Pokémon, competitive battles require:

  • Synergy among teammates
  • Speed control
  • Defensive planning
  • a clear win condition
  • Reliable answers to common threats
  • Pokémon assigned roles that complement each other

Checklist

Teams are not easy to build. Having a general teambuilding checklist to add structure can sometimes help in choosing teammates. A team should cover these aspects:

  • Decide what is your win condition(s)
  • Have a generalized main strategy (gimmick)
  • Choose 1-2 supporting Pokémon
  • Assign items to reinforce roles
  • Terastallization needs
  • Playtest and refine

Choosing your Pokémon

When choosing Pokémon, they should fill a role and support your overall strategy.
The key is to build outward from a starting point.

Start With a Core Idea

  • Build a team around your favorite Pokémon
  • Build a team around your strongest in-game Pokémon
  • Build around a popular duo with strong synergy
    • Support + sweeper duo
    • Redirection + set-up
  • Explore a known strategy/gimmick you use in-game

Build Around Roles

Assigning roles helps you understand your team's needs more clearly and narrow down options as you proceed.
Ask yourself:

  • Offensive Role: Who will deal damage?
  • Support Role: Who provides support?
  • Bulky pivots: Who can take hits?
  • Defensive answers: Who helps cover weaknesses/top-threats?

Teams Need a Strategy/Gimmick

Successful teams have a clear plan to win. This plan is your main strategy or gimmick that makes your team unique.

Common Strategies

  • Speed Control: Win by moving first and hitting hard
  • Trick Room Teams: Reverse turn order so slower Pokémon move first
  • Bulky Offensive: Use strong, tanky Pokémon that hit hard and live long
  • Weather Teams: Take advantage of weather effects to enhance a Pokémon's ability or moves
  • Terrain Boost: Take advantage of terrain effects to enhance a Pokémon's ability or moves

Common Gimmicks

  • Ability-based Gimmick:
    • Intimidate
    • Guts + Flame Orb
    • Protosynthesis / Quirk Drive + Booster Energy
  • Move-based Gimmick:
    • Fake Out
    • Rest + Chesto Berry
    • Follow Me + Setup move

Understanding Item Choice

Held items help enhance a Pokémon's performance in its role. Depending on the item, it can either boost speed, power, survivability, or a niche utility.
Ask yourself:

  • What is this Pokémon's role?
  • Does it need more damge? More bulk? More speed?
  • Does this item support the team's overall strategy?
  • Is this item needed to cover niche situations?

Types of Items

  • Offensive Item Examples:
    • Choice Scarf/Specs/Band
    • Life Orb
  • Defensive Item Examples:
    • Assult Vest
    • Lum Berry
  • Utility Item Examples:
    • Eviolite
    • Covert Cloak

What Tera to Use

Terastallization is the game mechanic introduced in Pokémon Scarlet and Violet, it is one of the most influential aspects of modern competitive play. Tera lets you transform your Pokémon's typing mid-battle, adding either offensive or defensive flexibility.

How to Assign Tera Types

  • Does the Pokémon need more of an offensive boost or defensive boost?
  • What typing(s) does your team struggle against?
  • Does it help you win matchups your team otherwise loses?
  • Does it support the team's primary win condition?
  • Is this tera needed often, or situational?

Tera Benefits

  • Boosts moves of same tera typing
  • Boosts offensive or defensive capabilities
  • Can give Pokemon immunities to typings
  • Removes a Pokémon's main weaknesses
  • Great for pivoting strategies
  • Gives your Pokémon surprise resistances
  • Improve matchups against threats
