Training Competitive Pokémon

Stats and Base Stat Total (BST)

Every Pokémon has six stats:

  • HP
  • Attack
  • Defense
  • Special Attack
  • Special Defense
  • Speed

These stats decide how bulky, hard-hitting, or fast a Pokémon is. Every Pokémon also has their own Base Stat Total (BST) that determines the Pokémon's overall potential without training. The higher the BST the more potential a Pokémon has per stat value.

What Affects a Pokémon's Stats

Pokémon VGC uses level 50 as the standard level for Pokémon in competitive format. At this level is where you want to fine-tune your Pokémon's stats to make them most viable in competitive play.

Components That Affect Stats

  • Base Stats: This is the Pokémon's natural build in game, this does not change.
  • IVs: A Pokémon's "potential" in each stat, think of this like its natural talent. Learn More
  • EVs: Training points that help shape a Pokémon's performance.
  • Nature: A personality trait that boost one stat by 10% and lowers another by 10%.

EVs

Effort Values (EVs) are training points you invest into specific stats of each Pokémon.

Fundamentals

  • A single Pokémon can have up to 510 total EVs.
  • Each stat can have at most 252 EVs.
    • At level 50: 252 EVs gives a stat ~31 stat points
    • At level 100: 252 EVs gives a stat 63 stat points

How to Increase EVs

Scarlet and Violet gives players fast, beginner-friendly EV training tools

  • Vitamin Items: Give 10 EVs in a stat
  • Feathers: Give 1 EV to a stat
  • Power Items: Held item, that gives +8 EVs per battle in a specific stat

How to Decrease EVs

Made a mistake? No worries, feeding Pokémon specific berries decreases a stat by 10 EVs.

Why Make EV Spreads

Creating EV spreads is how you go from "I have a strong Pokémon" to "I've built an absolute monster."

Benefits

  • Survive key hits
  • Outspeed threats
  • Improve roles
  • Reliable results

General EV Spreads

  • Offensive: Max Attack / Sp. Atk. + max Speed
  • Defensive: Max HP + max Defense / Sp. Def.
