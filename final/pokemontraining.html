Training Competitive Pokemon

Stats and Base Stat Total (BST)

Every Pokemon has six stats:

  • HP
  • Attack
  • Defense
  • Special Attack
  • Special Defense
  • Speed

These stats decide how bulky, hard-hitting, or fast a pokemon is. Every Pokemon also has their own Base Stat Total (BST) that determines the Pokemon's overall potential without training. The higher the BST the more potential a Pokemon has per stat value.

What Effects a Pokemon's Stats

Pokemon VGC uses level 50 as the standard level for Pokemon in Competitive Format. At this level is where you want to finetune your Pokemon's stats to make them most viable in Competitive play.

Components That Effect Stats

  • Base Stats: This is the Pokemon's natural build in game, this does not change.
  • IV's: A Pokemon's "potential" in each stat, think of this like its natural talent. Learn More
  • EV's: Training pooints that help shape a Pokemon's performance.
  • Nature: A personality trait that boost one stat by 10% and lowers another by 10%.

EV's

Effort Values (EV's) are training points you invest into specific stats of each Pokemon.

Fundamentals

  • A single Pokemon can have up to 510 total EVs
  • Each stat can have at most 252 EVs
    • At level 50: 252 EVs in a stat gives ~31 stat points
    • At level 100: 252 EVs in a stat gives 63 stat points

How to Increase EV's

Scarlet and Violet gives players fast, beginner-friendly EV training tools

  • Vitamin Items: Give 10 EVs in a stat
  • Feathers: Give 1 EV to a stat
  • Power Items: Held item, that gives +8 EVs per battle in a specific stat

How to Decrease EV's

Made a mistake? No worries, feeding Pokemon specific berries decreases a stat by 10 EV's

Why Make EV Spreads

Creating EV spreads is how you go from "have a strong Pokemon" to "I've built an absolute monster."

Benefits

  • Survive key hits
  • Outspeed Threats
  • Improve roles
  • Reliable results

General EV Spreads

  • Offensive: Max Attack / Sp.Atk. + max speed
  • Defnesive: Max HP + max Defense / Sp.Def.
