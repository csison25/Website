Breeding Competitive Pokémon

Why Breed Pokémon?

Breeding gives players full control. Instead of depending on luck with a wild-caught Pokémon, you can breed for specific stats, nature, ability, and moves. This level of control is especially valuable in ensuring your team is consistent and reliable in competitive play. In VGC, the difference between winning and losing is razor-thin: a single speed point, a nature that boosts the right stat, or an inherited egg move that enables a key strategy. Breeding enables consistency, allowing players to build strategies with confidence and predict outcomes more accurately. It's like building your own dream athletes from the ground up!

What are IVs

IVs (Individual Values) are hidden numbers for each stat:

  • HP
  • Attack
  • Defense
  • Special Attack
  • Special Defense
  • Speed

Tip:

Some competitive builds prefer a low IV in a stat

  • Physical Attackers: Don't need Special Attack IVs
  • Trick Room Teams: Want Pokémon as slow as possible

IV Judge Function

Once unlocked, usually after beating the main story, the Judge Function appears in your PC boxes. Each stat has a phrase next to it indicating the amount of IVs in that stat. The stats of regularly caught Pokémon are usually not the "best," highlighting the importance of breeding. This function allows you to quickly check which parents are ideal for breeding and track progress toward creating top competitive Pokémon.

Benefits of Breeding Pokémon

Breeding lets players:

  • Optimize stats: Create Pokémon with perfect or near-perfect IVs.
  • Pass down the correct nature: Pass down natures that suit a Pokémon's role.
  • Obtain Egg Moves: Get moves that the Pokémon would not otherwise learn through level-up.
  • Inherit an ability/hidden ability: You need to have a parent with the desired ability.
  • Create other copies: Once you have excellent parent Pokémon, you can produce additional offspring for different roles.

How to Start Breeding

This explanation will be for Pokémon Scarlet and Violet; other generations will be the same, but without the picnic system.

Step-by-step:

  1. Choose a base Pokémon - Catch a wild Pokémon with decent IVs that you want to make competitive.
  2. Pair it with a good IV partner - Usually this would be a high-IV Ditto, and put both Pokémon in your party.
  3. Equip Item - Give the high-IV partner or Ditto the Destiny Knot. This will ensure that 5 IVs from either partner are passed down.
  4. Set up a Picnic - Start a picnic in-game, wait a little bit, then check the picnic basket for eggs.
  5. Eat a meal - Make sandwich that provides Egg Power boost. This will increase number of eggs produced.
  6. Swap out base Pokémon - As you hatch the eggs, switch out the base Pokémon with offspring that have more optimal IVs and nature.
  7. Equip Item - After you produce an offspring with the desired IVs and nature, equip them with the Everstone. This guarantees the holder's nature is always inherited.

Essentials:

  • Access to the Day Care (Depends on the game)
    • Pokémon Scarlet and Violet use the Picnic System as the "Day Care," just need two compatible. Pokémon in your party
  • Two compatible Pokémon - Same egg group, or Pokémon + Ditto
    • For same egg group breeding, the female Pokémon is the base Pokémon (offspring).
  • Destiny knot - Passes IVs
  • Everstone - Passes the nature
  • Flame Body - Pokémon ability, halves amount of steps to hatch an egg
  • Judge Function - Check IVs

Hatching Eggs

Hatching the eggs is very simple. Put the eggs in your party and move around. To hatch eggs faster:

  • Have a Pokémon with the ability Flame Body or Magma Armor in your party.
  • Use in-game movement mechanics to increase frequency of steps.
    • Pokémon Scarlet and Violet: Ride Koraidon or Miraidon around Paldea
    • Previous Generations: Ride the bicycle
  • Optimize time by choosing routes in the game that allow you to "loop" or are continuous in a single direction.
